Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California. Apple is the largest technology company by revenue (totaling US$365.8 billion in 2021). As of June 2022, Apple is the world's biggest company by market capitalization, the fourth-largest personal computer vendor by unit sales and second-largest mobile phone manufacturer. It is one of the Big Five American information technology companies, alongside Alphabet (known for Google), Amazon, Meta (known for Facebook), and Microsoft.

Definition of the terms:

Open Price: The open price refers to the price at which a stock begins trading at the beginning of a trading session or market day. It is the first recorded price of a stock during that particular trading day.

High Price: The high price represents the highest price at which a stock is traded during a specific trading session or market day. It indicates the peak value reached by the stock's price within that time period.

Low Price: The low price represents the lowest price at which a stock is traded during a specific trading session or market day. It indicates the minimum value reached by the stock's price within that time period.

Close Price: The close price, also known as the closing price, is the final price at which a stock is traded at the end of a trading session or market day. It is the last recorded price of a stock during that particular trading day.

Adjusted Close Price: The adjusted close price takes into account factors such as stock splits, dividends, and other corporate actions that may affect the stock's price. It provides a more accurate representation of the stock's historical performance by adjusting for these events.

Volume: Volume refers to the total number of shares or contracts traded during a specific trading session or market day. It measures the level of activity and liquidity in a stock. Higher volume generally indicates greater market interest and participation.
